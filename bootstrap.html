Mix n Max

Mix & Max is an app designed to take the guesswork out of looking your best, Everyone wants to put their best foot forward everyday and for many people that means dressing to impress. However, many of us struggle everyday to find clothing that looks great on our bodies and coordinates with each other. Mix & Max helps us mix up clothing already in our wardroble to create stunning new outfits.

Role, Team, & Time

This project was created by a team of three, my role being one of the UX designers. Completed in 3 weeks.

Tools

Figma, Miro, Canva

Research

We first needed to understand the relationship people have with their clothing and what is causing them pain. We were able to do this by conducting interviews, along with having a survey. Our results told us...

People have difficulties finding styles for their body type, people struggle to match items, creating outfits are time consuming, people dress to look good and equally for the weather. Along with that personal taste matters most compared to fast fashion.

From affinity mapping, we concluded and found that there could be two types of people who may need this app. One person who loves fashion and have developed their own sense of style, and the other who dress solely for comfort and could care less about fashion.

Definition

People with stylization issues struggle to create outfit combinations. How can we assist people in maximizing their items to create new outfit combinations to achieve the image that they want to give to the world?

Prototypes

Between three teammates we created prototypes based on our userflows for each feature. We then conducted user tests to test our prototypes combined.

For our user testings we created three tasks that include: sign up, add an item to your closet, and to start a chat. From the user tests, we found these key results:

Clarification needed with the VR scanning process Confusing button layout in sign-up/sign in screen
No way to return to main menu & indications about how to take photos needed
Conflictions with AI and live-chat. Also there was no clear way to end chat.
Max had a friendly tone of voice, and the character was unique.
>
Conclusion

People do not want to buy thousands of new clothes, they want to have clothes and create multiple outfits from multiple combinations. They also want to do this in a short amount of time. An app that helps to have a more effficient wardrobe using tech such as augmented reality and artificial intelligence.
